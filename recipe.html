Crepe Ingredeints:

  • 1 cup of oat flour (you can make this by blending rolled oats)
  • 1 scoop of your favorite protein powder (vanilla or unflavored works well)
  • 1 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup of almond milk (or any milk of your choice)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of honey or maple syrup (optional for sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Cooking spray or a small amount of oil for the pan


    • for fillings:

  • Greek yogurt
  • Fresh berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, etc.)
  • Sliced bananas
  • Honey or maple syrup for drizzling (optional)


    • Instructions:


  • Prepare the Batter: In a blender, combine the oat flour, protein powder, Greek yogurt, almond milk, eggs, honey (if using), vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Blend until you have a smooth batter. If the batter is too thick, you can add a little more almond milk to achieve the desired consistency.

  • Let the Batter Rest: Allow the batter to rest for about 10-15 minutes. This helps the oat flour absorb the liquid and makes for a better texture in your crepes.

  • Cook the Crepes: Heat a non-stick skillet or crepe pan over medium heat. Lightly grease it with cooking spray or a small amount of oil. Pour a small amount of batter (about 1/4 cup) into the pan and quickly swirl it around to spread it thinly and evenly. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the edges start to lift and the bottom is lightly golden. Flip the crepe and cook for another 1-2 minutes on the other side. Repeat this process for the remaining batter, adding a bit more oil to the pan as needed.

  • Assemble the Crepes: Spread Greek yogurt on each crepe. Add fresh berries and sliced bananas on top of the yogurt. Optionally, drizzle honey or maple syrup for extra sweetness.

  • Serve: Fold the crepes or roll them up, and serve immediately while they're warm.

    • These high-protein crepes are not only delicious but also provide a good dose of protein from the Greek yogurt and protein powder. Plus, they're versatile, so you can customize the filling to your liking with different fruits and toppings. Enjoy!




    For the Grilled Chicken:

    Ingredients:

  • In a bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, black pepper, and lemon juice.
  • Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over them.
  • Seal the bag or cover the dish and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes (or up to 24 hours for maximum flavor).
  • Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  • Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill for about 6-8 minutes per side or until the chicken is cooked through and has nice grill marks. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C).
  • Remove from the grill and let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing.

    • For the Quinoa:

  • Rinse the quinoa under cold water in a fine-mesh strainer.
  • In a saucepan, bring 2 cups of water or chicken broth to a boil.
  • Stir in the quinoa and a pinch of salt.
  • Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender.
  • Fluff the quinoa with a fork and keep it warm.

    • For the Roasted Vegetables:

  • Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  • Cut the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces and place them on a baking sheet.
  • Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with salt, black pepper, and dried thyme. Toss to coat.
  • Roast in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.

    • To Serve:

  • Divide the cooked quinoa among serving plates.
  • Top with slices of grilled chicken.
  • Serve with a generous portion of roasted vegetables on the side.

    • This grilled chicken with quinoa and roasted vegetables is not only high in protein but also packed with flavor and nutrients. It makes for a satisfying and healthy meal. Enjoy!














    Grilled Salmon

    Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (6-8 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

    • Prepare the Marinade:

  • 1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. This will be your marinade.

    • Marinate the Salmon:

  • 2. Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish or a large resealable plastic bag.
  • 3. Pour the marinade over the salmon fillets, ensuring they are well coated.
  • 4. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. You can marinate for longer if you have the time, up to 2 hours.

    • Preheat the Grill:

  • 5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 400-450°F or 200-230°C). Make sure to clean and oil the grill grates to prevent sticking.

    • Grill the Salmon:

  • 6. Remove the salmon from the marinade and let any excess drip off.
  • 7. Place the salmon fillets skin-side down on the grill.
  • 8. Grill for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets. You want the salmon to be opaque and easily flake with a fork. Be careful not to overcook, as salmon can dry out quickly.

    • Serve:

  • 9. Carefully remove the grilled salmon from the grill using a spatula. You can leave the skin on the grill if it sticks.
  • 10. Transfer the grilled salmon fillets to a serving platter.
  • 11. Garnish with lemon wedges and fresh parsley, if desired.

    • Serve the grilled salmon hot with your choice of side dishes, such as steamed vegetables, rice, or a salad. This grilled salmon recipe is not only high in protein but also packed with flavor from the marinade. It's a healthy and delicious meal that's perfect for lunch or dinner. Enjoy!





    High-Protein Stir-Fry Tofu and Broccoli



















    Ingredients:

  • 1 block of extra-firm tofu, cubed

  • 2 cups broccoli florets

  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced

  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced

  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced

  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar

  • 1 tablespoon honey or agave nectar

  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

  • 2 tablespoons water

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil for stir-frying

  • Cooked brown rice or quinoa for serving

    • Instructions

  • Press the tofu: Place the tofu between two clean kitchen towels and place a heavy object on top (like a cast iron pan). Let it press for about 15-20 minutes to remove excess water. Then, cut the tofu into cubes.

  • In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey or agave nectar, cornstarch, and water to make the sauce. Set it aside.

  • Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

  • Add the tofu cubes to the skillet and stir-fry until they turn golden brown on all sides. Remove the tofu from the skillet and set it aside.

  • In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  • Add the sliced onion, broccoli florets, and bell peppers to the skillet. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften but are still crisp.

  • Return the cooked tofu to the skillet with the vegetables.

  • Pour the sauce over the tofu and vegetables. Stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and coats everything evenly.

  • Serve the stir-fry over cooked brown rice or quinoa.
